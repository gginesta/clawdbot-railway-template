## Radical Idea #1: APAC Pest Control — The Overlooked Geography

### The Insight
While everyone is focused on US pest control consolidation (Rentokil/Terminix integration struggles, PE multiple compression), APAC is the highest-growth pest market:
- APAC pest control market: $6.15B (2025), **8.5% CAGR** (vs. 6.1% US/global)
- Asia Pacific reaching $7.85B by 2032
- APAC insect pest control products: $3.78B (2024) → $7.38B by 2033 — 7.72% CAGR
- Hong Kong: Guillermo is based there — management oversight much easier
- Southeast Asia: Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand, Vietnam — urbanizing rapidly; termite pressure high; climate-driven demand; largely unbranded, fragmented local operators
- Australia: Mature market with professional operators; fewer PE buyers; termite treatment is a recurring annual service due to high termite risk

**Acquisition math in APAC:**
- Australian pest control company: A$8-15M revenue, 25-28% EBITDA; buyers include Rentokil/Initial but many mid-market companies not yet acquired
- Entry multiples: Typically 4-5x EBITDA in Australia (less PE competition than US)
- Thai/SEA pest control: Very fragmented; no established multiples; could acquire at 3-4x SDE
- Example: A$12M revenue company in Sydney/Melbourne, 26% margin = A$3.1M EBITDA at 4.5x = A$14M (~$9M USD)
- $50M USD: 2-3 platforms in Australia + SEA, organic growth in high-CAGR markets

**Why this is radical:**
- US focus is the obvious trade. APAC is less efficient, lower multiples, higher growth rates
- Guillermo's Hong Kong base = management oversight advantage
- Climate change creating extreme pest pressure in tropical zones

**Score adjustment from base pest control:**
- Entry multiple: Lower than US (4-4.5x APAC vs. 5-6x US) → slightly higher yield
- Growth: 8.5% CAGR vs. 6.1% US → higher distributions by Yr5
- Currency risk: AUD/USD, SGD/USD are relatively stable
- Execution risk: Cultural/regulatory adaptation needed
- **Net: Yield +1-2% vs. US pest control; score approximately 81-83**

## Radical Idea #2: "Death Care Spectrum" — Funeral + Cremation Direct + Pet Cremation

### The Insight
Traditional funeral homes are under pricing pressure from cremation. But what if you OWN the cremation wave?

**Foundation Partners Group (FPG) strategy:**
- FPG built a "cremation-first" death care platform
- Lighter asset base than SCI (no cemeteries, less real estate)
- Focused on cremation + digital memorial services
- PE-backed; building toward exit
- Key insight: Direct cremation companies (Tulip, Neptune Society) are growing fast

**The radical combination:**
1. **Buy traditional funeral homes** at 5.5x EBITDA (existing high-service clientele; boomers prefer)
2. **Add cremation direct service** under a separate brand (captures cost-conscious customers)
3. **Add pet cremation** as a service line (entirely recession-proof; pets are family; growing market)
   - Pet cremation: $500M+ US market; fragmented; growing 6-8%/year
   - Margins: 40-60% EBITDA for pet cremation (no embalming, smaller facilities, subscription wellness plans)
   - Entry multiples: 4-6x EBITDA for pet cremation (less competition than human funeral)

**Combined "Death Care Ecosystem" Portfolio:**
- 8 traditional funeral homes ($2.5M revenue each = $20M combined) at 5.5x EBITDA = $33M
- 4 pet cremation facilities ($500K revenue each = $2M combined) at 5x EBITDA = $7.5M
- Direct cremation brand (build or buy): $2M revenue at 4x EBITDA = $5M
- Total deployed: $45.5M
- Combined EBITDA: (8 × $700K) + (4 × $200K) + $400K = $6.4M
- FCF at 85%: $5.44M → **12% yield Year 3**
- Year 5 with growth: $8M+ EBITDA → **FCF 17%**

**Note:** Lower Yr3 yield vs. pure pest control (12% vs. 14%), but SUPERIOR recession protection and demographic tailwind
**Score:** ~76-78 (close to funeral home base; pet cremation adds upside)

## Near-Miss: Septic Services

### Why This Deserves a Second Look
- Septic pumping/inspection: Essential service (regulatory requirement in most states for septic systems)
- Recurring: Every 3-5 years inspection requirement + emergency calls
- Georgia Oak Partners + Source Capital invested in Septic Blue (Atlanta/Charlotte/Raleigh)
- Real case: Dan Spracklin bought $1M/year septic business → grew to $3M in 2 years at 30% margins
- Entry multiples: ~3-4x EBITDA (less PE competition than pest control)
- EBITDA margins: 25-35% for efficient operators

**$50M Math for septic platform:**
- Platform at $8M revenue, 28% EBITDA = $2.24M EBITDA at 4.5x = $10M
- 8 bolt-on acquisitions at 3x EBITDA ($500K EBITDA each) = $12M
- Year 3: $10M+ revenue combined; FCF $3.5M+ on $22M = **16% yield**
- Challenge: Limited to rural/suburban markets; different demographic than urban pest control
- Lower total addressable market: US septic systems ~1/3 of households
- **Score: ~70** — interesting but limited geography

## Portfolio Strategy Analysis: 3x $15M vs. 1x $45M

### Option A: Single Platform ($45M + $5M reserves)
**Target:** One pest control company at $25M revenue, 28% margin = $7M EBITDA at 6.5x = $45.5M
- Yr3 FCF: $5.95M → **12% yield**
- Risk: Concentration in one operator, one market
- Upside: Cleaner management, faster decisions, one CEO to hire

### Option B: Three Deals (~$16M each)
**Split:** Pest Control ($16M) + Funeral Homes ($17M) + Fire Protection ($17M)
- Pest control: $8M rev, 28% = $2.24M EBITDA at 6x = $13.4M; FCF Yr3 $1.9M
- Funeral homes: 4 locations × $3.8M = $15.2M; EBITDA $2.8M; FCF $2.4M
- Fire protection: $10M rev, 22% = $2.2M EBITDA at 7.5x = $16.5M; FCF $1.8M
- Combined FCF Yr3: $6.1M on $45.1M → **13.5% yield**
- Risk: 3 management teams, 3 integration challenges
- Upside: Geographic and sector diversification; different recession responses

### Option C: Platform + Bolt-On Machine (Recommended)
**Best structure:** One strong pest control platform ($25M) + 5-6 pest control bolt-ons ($25M)
- Superior multiple arbitrage (platform at 6x, bolt-ons at 3.5-4x)
- Operational synergies (shared chemicals, routes, marketing)
- Yr3 FCF: $7M+ on $50M = **14% yield**
- Year 5: $10M+ FCF → **20%+ yield**
- Single sector focus = easier to hire expert CEO

**Verdict: Option C wins on yield AND operational simplicity**
