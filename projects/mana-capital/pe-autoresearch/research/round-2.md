## Deep Dive Results

### 1. Pest Control Services (Refined Score)

**Real Transaction Data:**
- Rentokil spent GBP199M on 34 pest control acquisitions in 2023 (avg ~GBP5.8M/acquisition = ~$7.3M)
- At GBP76M in acquired revenue, implied ~2.6x revenue multiple
- Small/mid-market: 3.26-6.5x EBITDA (Peak Business Valuation); $10-50M revenue businesses: 8-12x EBITDA
- Terminix expanding to Latin America (Brazil, Mexico) — Dec 2024 signal
- 2024 M&A: "one of most prolific M&A sectors" (Capstone); increased competition for assets = multiple pressure

**$50M Deployment Math:**
- Strategy A: $45M to acquire 3-4 mid-sized pest control platforms ($8-12M revenue each) at ~5-6x EBITDA
- Each platform: $8M rev, 28% EBITDA margin = $2.24M EBITDA; at 6x = $13.4M acquisition price
- 3 platforms = ~$40M deployed, $6.72M combined EBITDA
- FCF at 85% conversion = $5.7M/year distributions from Yr 3 (post-integration)
- Yield on $50M = 11.4% (conservative; pre-leverage)
- With modest leverage (2x): yield increases to 15-18% on equity
- Strategy B: One $45M platform + bolt-ons = more concentrated but higher quality
- Organic growth: sector growing 6.1-6.5% CAGR; bolt-ons at 3-4x EBITDA add yield

**Geographic opportunities:**
- US: Most mature, highest M&A competition, but deep talent pool
- LatAm: Terminix entering Brazil/Mexico signals opportunity; multiples lower; climate drives pest volumes
- APAC: Rentokil active; lower multiples vs. US; Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand have active markets
- EU: Anticimex active consolidator; fragmented Southern European market

**Revised Score:**
- Yield: 15% Yr3 (conservative, no leverage) → score 75; with leverage → 82
- Predictability: 70-85% recurring → score 83
- Entry multiple: 5-6x realistic for quality mid-market → score 80
- Operator risk: Specialist but robust labor market → 68
- Recession resilience: Essential, held well 2008 & 2020 → 87
- Scalability: Bolt-on machine; Rentokil model proven → 90
- **Revised Composite: 0.35×75 + 0.20×83 + 0.15×80 + 0.10×68 + 0.10×87 + 0.10×90 = 79.5**
- Note: Dropping slightly because multiples have risen due to PE competition; LatAm/APAC can compensate

### 2. Healthcare Revenue Cycle Management (RCM)

**Real Transaction Data:**
- Scope Research: sub-$1M EBITDA companies at 3-6x; $1-10M EBITDA at 6-10x
- One disclosed example: $3.5M revenue, $1.2M EBITDA sold at 1.4x revenue / 4x EBITDA (smaller deal)
- Tech-enabled RCM: 20x+ EBITDA (too expensive, out of scope)
- Services-only RCM ($5-20M revenue, manual/outsourced): 5-8x EBITDA range
- EBITDA margins: 20-30% for services-only; 30-40% for tech-augmented
- FCF conversion: ~90% (minimal capex — just labor and software)
- US outsourced RCM market: growing to $272B by 2030 (GlobeNewsWire, 2025)
- Key growth driver: Healthcare provider shortage → outsourcing; AI-driven efficiency → expanding margins

**$50M Deployment Math:**
- Buy 2-3 services-only RCM companies ($15-25M revenue, $3-5M EBITDA each) at 6-7x
- Example: $20M rev, 25% margin = $5M EBITDA; at 7x = $35M purchase
- FCF at 90% = $4.5M/year → 12.9% yield (no leverage)
- Second deal: $12M rev, 22% margin = $2.64M EBITDA; at 6x = $15.8M
- Combined: $6.64M EBITDA → FCF $5.98M from $50M deployed → **11.9% yield**

**Key concern:** 
- Geographically limited — primarily US-only opportunity (healthcare billing is US-centric)
- AI disruption risk: Automation reducing headcount → good for margins, but could commoditize the service
- Client concentration: Hospital systems → could change vendors or insource
- Regulatory risk: Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement changes

**Geographic fit:**
- Poor fit for LatAm/APAC (US healthcare billing is domestic)
- EU health systems are government-run — different billing landscape
- Partial: Some offshore RCM companies serving US providers from India/Philippines — but this is a different business model

**Revised Score:**
- Yield: 12% unlevered → score 63
- Predictability: 90%+ recurring → score 88
- Entry multiple: 6-7x → score 70
- Operator risk: Needs healthcare billing expertise; talent depth in US → 58
- Recession resilience: Healthcare billing doesn't stop → 82
- Scalability: Geographic rollup limited to US; bolt-ons possible → 60
- **Revised Composite: 0.35×63 + 0.20×88 + 0.15×70 + 0.10×58 + 0.10×82 + 0.10×60 = 70.3**
- Note: Good business but geography constraint (US-only) hurts ranking vs. global mandate

### 3. Funeral Homes / Death Care

**Real Transaction Data:**
- 28,000+ US funeral homes, $16B+ industry; majority still family-owned
- ~50% of funeral home owners plan to retire by 2028 (NFDA 2023 survey)
- PE active: Rosewood Private Investments acquired Milestone 2024; active consolidation in Western MA
- Typical EBITDA multiples: 5-8x independent (Peak Business Valuation); SCI and Carriage Services as public comps trade at premium
- SCI stock doubled 2020-2024; 30,000 more seniors died in 2024 vs 2023 (+1.3%)
- EBITDA margins: 20-35% for independents; 35-45% for corporately-owned
- FCF conversion: ~85%
- Pre-need revenue: 20-30% locked in advance — provides annuity-like base
- Only 16% of consumers ever look at more than 1 funeral home → local monopoly

**$50M Deployment Math:**
- Buy portfolio of 15-20 independent funeral homes (avg $2M revenue, $500K EBITDA each) at 5.5x
- Per acquisition: $500K EBITDA × 5.5x = $2.75M
- 18 acquisitions × $2.75M = $49.5M deployed
- Combined EBITDA: 18 × $500K = $9M
- FCF at 85% = $7.65M distributions → **15.3% yield**
- OR buy 3-4 multi-chapel operators at 6x EBITDA ($4-5M EBITDA each) = better management leverage
- 3 operators × $4.5M EBITDA × 6x = $81M — slightly over budget; adjust to 2 operators = $54M
- 2 operators: $9M EBITDA × 85% = $7.65M FCF → 14.2% yield on $54M

**Key advantages:**
- Near-monopoly local pricing power
- Aging boomer demographic (structural tailwind 15-20 years)
- Cremation shift opens pricing models (pre-need becomes more flexible)
- Low technology disruption risk
- Funeral homes often own their real estate (additional value)

**Geographic opportunities:**
- US primary (most mature PE activity)
- Latin America: Brazil, Mexico, Chile — fragmented, underdeveloped, but cultural differences matter
- APAC: Japan (aging population, cultural traditions), but complex entry

**Revised Score:**
- Yield: 14-15% → score 73
- Predictability: Demographic guarantee + pre-need contracts → 82
- Entry multiple: 5.5-6x → score 78
- Operator risk: Needs funeral director licenses; specialized → 55
- Recession resilience: Death is recession-proof → 97
- Scalability: Geographic bolt-on; efficiency gains from shared services → 68
- **Revised Composite: 0.35×73 + 0.20×82 + 0.15×78 + 0.10×55 + 0.10×97 + 0.10×68 = 76.5**

### 4. Accounting/CPA Firms

**Real Transaction Data:**
- Lower middle market: 3.5-6x revenue (1x-1.5x revenue is old method, now EBITDA-driven for PE)
- EBITDA multiple: varies; one deal example from Jetpack = 4x non-adjusted EBITDA for $3M revenue firm
- PE-backed rollups: Onex, Parthenon Capital, Apax Partners building accounting platforms
- Larger firm deals: 7-11x EBITDA (confirmed by Accounting Firm Acquired for 11X article)
- EBITDA margins: 25-40% after normalizing partner comp
- FCF conversion: 85-90%
- Recurring revenue: 70-80% (tax + bookkeeping clients are sticky, multi-year)
- Key disruption risk: AI-assisted accounting tools (QuickBooks AI, Xero, etc.) → some tasks automated
- Key opportunity: Offshoring labor to India/Philippines dramatically expands margins

**$50M Deployment Math:**
- Buy 10-15 CPA firms ($3-8M revenue each) at 5x EBITDA
- Target: $5M revenue firm with 30% normalized EBITDA = $1.5M EBITDA at 5x = $7.5M acquisition
- 6 acquisitions = $45M deployed; combined EBITDA $9M; FCF $7.65M → 15.3% yield
- Post-offshoring play: push EBITDA margins to 40% = $12M EBITDA → FCF $10.2M → **20.4% yield**

**Key risk:**
- Partner flight: If founding partners leave, client churn can be significant
- Earnout structures common — cash upfront is less than headline
- AI disruption: Legitimate threat to compliance-heavy work

**Geographic opportunities:**
- US/UK dominant but EU feasible (Germany, Netherlands professional services)
- Latin America: More fragmented, lower margins, some regulatory complexity

**Revised Score:**
- Yield: 15% unlevered (offshoring upside to 20%) → score 73
- Predictability: 75% recurring → 75
- Entry multiple: 5x → 80
- Operator risk: Partner dependency is the key risk → 48
- Recession resilience: Tax/audit doesn't go away → 80
- Scalability: Rollup possible but partner integration is slow → 62
- **Revised Composite: 0.35×73 + 0.20×75 + 0.15×80 + 0.10×48 + 0.10×80 + 0.10×62 = 72.0**

### 5. Commercial Cleaning/Facilities Management

**Real Transaction Data:**
- Janitorial/cleaning typically 3-5x EBITDA for lower-middle market (BizBuySell benchmarks)
- PE interest growing but margins are thin: 10-18% EBITDA for most commercial cleaners
- Recurring contracts: 70-80% of revenue from ongoing commercial agreements
- Labor = 60-70% of costs → high wage inflation risk
- FragmentED market: Top 10 companies <30% of US market
- One major risk: Workers' comp, labor law compliance, high turnover

**Key challenge:** The low margins (10-15%) mean even at 4x EBITDA, the yield math is:
- Buy at 4x EBITDA on $1.5M (from $10M revenue, 15% margin) = $6M
- FCF at 80% = $1.2M on $6M invested = 20% gross yield per deal
- But scaling this to $50M means managing many companies with thin margins and labor risk
- Labor shortages and wage inflation are structural headwinds

**Revised Score:**
- Yield: 18% per deal at small scale, but management complexity at $50M scale → 68
- Predictability: 75% contracted → 72
- Entry multiple: 4-5x → 85
- Operator risk: Labor management intensive → 40
- Recession resilience: Essential but discretionary portions cut → 72
- Scalability: Hard to scale without strong management team → 50
- **Revised Composite: 0.35×68 + 0.20×72 + 0.15×85 + 0.10×40 + 0.10×72 + 0.10×50 = 67.1**
- Note: Dropping in ranking; labor risk is too high for indefinite hold

### 6. Laundromats (Portfolio Approach)

**Real Transaction Data:**
- EBITDA multiples: 3.44-4.85x (Peak Business Valuation)
- Walker Deibel (leading buy-side advisor) calls it "bond-like returns at premium prices" — a warning
- Average laundromat: $250K-$700K revenue, $80-150K EBITDA
- $50M would buy ~150-200 individual laundromats — operationally impossible
- Platform approach: Build 30-50 unit cluster in 2-3 cities
- At 4x EBITDA on 40 laundromats ($100K EBITDA each) = $16M invested for $4M EBITDA = 25% gross yield
- But: Equipment replacement (washers/dryers) = ~$50K per unit every 7-10 years
- True FCF = ~70-75% of EBITDA after maintenance capex
- Adjusted yield: $3M FCF on $16M = 18.75% → excellent but requires 40 units

**Key challenges:**
- $50M at 4x means deploying across 100+ individual units → massive management overhead
- Geographic clustering essential; but $50M too much for one city
- Competition from app-based wash-and-fold services (Rinse, Poplin)
- No clear "platform" architecture at $50M scale
- Not truly recurring: customers are habitual but not contracted

**Revised Score:**
- Yield: 18% (gross) → 20% if rent-optimized, BUT true FCF after capex → 75
- Predictability: Habitual but not contracted → 52
- Entry multiple: 4x → 88
- Operator risk: Needs regional managers, equipment specialists → 52
- Recession resilience: Essential → 80
- Scalability: Fundamentally unit-economics-limited at $50M → 40
- **Revised Composite: 0.35×75 + 0.20×52 + 0.15×88 + 0.10×52 + 0.10×80 + 0.10×40 = 66.9**
- Dropping: $50M scale makes this operationally untenable

- EBITDA margins: 20-30% for waste collection; higher for specialty
- FCF conversion: ~75% (vehicle fleet capex drag)
