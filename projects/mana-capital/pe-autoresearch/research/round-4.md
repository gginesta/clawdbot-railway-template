## Acquisition Profile #1: Pest Control Platform (PRIMARY RECOMMENDATION)

### Target Profile
- **Revenue:** $8-20M
- **EBITDA:** $2.2-5.5M (28% target margin)
- **Employees:** 40-120 technicians
- **Structure:** Founder-led or family-owned, not PE-backed
- **Characteristics:** 75%+ recurring monthly/quarterly service contracts; residential + commercial mix; <2% monthly attrition; no single customer >10% of revenue
- **Ideal geography:** US Sun Belt (Texas, Florida, Southeast) OR Latin America (Colombia, Chile, Mexico)

### Entry Valuation Math
**US Strategy:**
- 1 platform at $15M revenue, 28% EBITDA = $4.2M EBITDA; at 6x = **$25.2M acquisition price**
- Remaining $24.8M deployed in 5-8 bolt-ons at 3.5-4x EBITDA ($800K-$1.2M EBITDA each = $2.8-4.8M per bolt-on)
- Year 1: Platform integration, add 3 bolt-ons ($4.5M deployed)
- Year 2-3: 4 more bolt-ons ($10M deployed); organic growth 6-8%/year

**LatAm Strategy (higher risk/higher return):**
- Acquire market leader in Colombia/Chile at 4-5x EBITDA (lower multiples than US)
- $10-15M revenue, 25% margin = $2.5-3.75M EBITDA; at 4.5x = $11.25-16.9M
- Replicate monthly contract model with proprietary chemicals
- 5-6 regional bolt-ons at 2-3x EBITDA (very fragmented market)

**Projected Yr3 Cash-on-Cash Yield: 14% (unlevered)**
**With 2x debt financing: 18-22%**

### Comparable Transactions
- Rentokil acquired 34 pest control companies in 2023 at avg $7.3M each (implied ~2.6x revenue)
- Riata Capital: Greenix platform acquired Insight Pest Solutions (May 2024), Spidexx (March 2025)
- Capstone: PE-backed buyers now 60% of pest control M&A; competition increasing
- Platform deal: Typical buy platform at 5-6x EBITDA; bolt-ons at 3-4x EBITDA

### Operator Hiring Plan
- Year 0-6 months: Founder stays (rollover equity incentive); hire Operations Director
- Year 6-18 months: Hire dedicated M&A/Integration Manager for bolt-on program
- Year 18 months: Transition to external CEO; budget $200-250K salary + equity
- CEO profile: Former VP Operations at Rollins, Rentokil, or Western Exterminator; or adjacent services GM

### Why This Beats Alternatives
- 25-35% EBITDA margins are rare in services
- 70-85% recurring revenue = annuity-like cash flows
- Market growing 6.1% CAGR → organic growth without new acquisitions
- Climate change expanding demand structurally
- Bolt-on arbitrage: buy at 3-4x, value at 6-8x platform → creates equity value AND increases cash flow

## Acquisition Profile #2: Funeral Home Portfolio (SECONDARY RECOMMENDATION)

### Target Profile
- **Revenue:** $1.5-5M per location
- **EBITDA:** $400K-$1.5M per location (26-31% margin per Johnson Consulting standards)
- **Employees:** 8-25 per location
- **Structure:** Retiring founder (age 55-70); family-owned for generations; NOT PE-backed
- **Characteristics:** Community-established brand (15+ years); own their real estate; mix of traditional + cremation; no single dependency owner
- **Ideal geography:** US (primary); Latin America feasible in Chile/Colombia (secondary)
- **Target count:** 15-20 individual locations (geographic clustering in 2-3 markets)

### Entry Valuation Math
- Average acquisition: $2.5M revenue, $700K EBITDA at 5.5x = **$3.85M per acquisition**
- 12 acquisitions = $46.2M deployed
- Combined EBITDA: 12 × $700K = $8.4M
- FCF at 85% (minimal capex for funeral homes): $7.14M/year from Yr3
- **Yr3 Cash-on-Cash Yield: 14.3%**

**Additional value: Real estate**
- Funeral homes typically own 3,000-8,000 sq ft in prime commercial locations
- 12 locations × ~$1.5M estimated real estate value = $18M embedded equity (not included in yield calc)
- Sale-leaseback option to recycle capital = could fund additional acquisitions

### Comparable Transactions
- Johnson Consulting Group: Standard EBITDA of 26-31% for healthy independent funeral home
- Creedy & Co: Typical PE multiple 4-6.5x EBITDA (current market)
- Park Lawn Corp: Taken private at $871M after 170 locations → validates roll-up model
- PE-backed consolidators active: Rosewood (Milestone acquisition 2024), multiple others

### Key Strategic Advantages
- Demographic inevitability: US deaths increasing 1-2%/year through 2040 (boomer cohort)
- 50% of owners retiring by 2028: supply of acquisition targets is exceptional
- Real estate value = balance sheet protection
- Pre-need insurance contracts create locked-in future revenue
- ZERO customer concentration risk
- Local monopoly dynamics (16% of consumers ever comparison shop)

### Operator Hiring Plan

- 3-6 months: Hire Funeral Home Group CEO (from SCI, Carriage, or Park Lawn alumni network)
- Budget: $250-400K compensation + equity for CEO with multi-location experience
- Licensed funeral directors: Existing staff retained; license as barrier to entry, not barrier to operations

## Acquisition Profile #3: Fire Protection Platform (TERTIARY)

### Target Profile
- **Revenue:** $10-25M
- **EBITDA:** $2-5M (20-22% margin; 25%+ for inspection-heavy)
- **Employees:** 30-100 certified technicians
- **Structure:** Founder-owned or founder-backed; NOT a platform already; inspection-focused (40%+ recurring)
- **Geography:** US regional leader; EU (UK, Netherlands, Nordics) as secondary
- **Key criteria:** NICET-certified technicians; strong inspection/ITM revenue mix (vs. installation-heavy)

### Entry Valuation Math
- Target: $18M revenue, 22% EBITDA = $3.96M EBITDA; at 7.5x = **$29.7M acquisition**
- Remaining $20.3M for 3-4 bolt-ons at 5-6x EBITDA ($600K EBITDA each = $3-3.6M per bolt-on)
- Combined at Yr3: $6M+ EBITDA; FCF at 82% = $4.9M

**Yr3 Cash-on-Cash Yield: ~10%**
**Note: Lower yield vs. pest control/funeral homes; justified by mandatory-compliance revenue and tech upside**

### Key Strategic Notes
- "Deficiency-to-repair" cycle: Every inspection generates service/repair referrals at 40-50% margins
- Fire protection generates $4 in service revenue for every $1 in inspection revenue (industry data)
- Platform premium at exit: Pye-Barker sold at 17-20x EBITDA → massive multiple arbitrage from 7-8x entry
- IoT/smart monitoring: Adding monitoring contracts converts inspection customers to MRR (25-45x MRR valuation)

### Re-Score with Concrete Numbers
- Yield 10% Yr3 → score 56
- Exit arbitrage potential (not primary metric but real upside) — scalability score bumped
- **Revised Composite: 70.9** (no change; numbers confirm estimate)

## Acquisition Profile #4: Waste Collection Operator (4th)

### Target Profile
- **Revenue:** $15-30M
- **EBITDA:** $3-6M (20-25% margin)
- **Employees:** 50-150
- **Structure:** Founder-owned regional hauler; NOT PE-backed
- **Key assets:** Municipal service contracts (2+ years remaining); modest vehicle fleet
- **Geography:** US regional (Midwest/Southeast fragmented areas) OR Latin America (growing middle class + urbanization driving waste management formalization)

### Entry Valuation Math
- $20M revenue, 22% margin = $4.4M EBITDA; at 7x = $30.8M
- 2 bolt-ons at 6x ($1.5M EBITDA each) = $18M
- Combined: $7.4M EBITDA; FCF at 75% (vehicle capex) = $5.55M
- **Yr3 Cash-on-Cash Yield: 11.1%**

### Key Challenge
- Vehicle fleet capex ($50K-200K per truck) creates ongoing capital requirements
- Municipal contract renewals every 3-7 years → bid uncertainty
- Lower yield than pest control/funeral due to capex drag
- Geography: LatAm opportunity in Colombia, Mexico, Chile where formal waste management is consolidating

## Acquisition Profile #5: CPA/Accounting Firm Portfolio (5th)

### Target Profile
- **Revenue:** $3-10M per firm
- **EBITDA:** $900K-3M (normalized at 30%)
- **Staff:** 15-50 professionals
- **Structure:** 2-3 partner firm; founding partners age 55+; transition-ready
- **Characteristics:** 70%+ recurring (tax + bookkeeping clients); diversified client base; no single client >10% revenue; some advisory/CFO-as-a-service work
- **Geography:** US (primary); UK (secondary); Australia/NZ (high potential with offshoring play)

### Entry Valuation Math
- Buy 6 firms averaging $6M revenue, 30% margin = $1.8M EBITDA at 5x = **$9M each**
- 6 firms = $54M → slightly over; adjust to 5 firms = $45M deployed
- 5 × $1.8M EBITDA = $9M combined; FCF at 88% = $7.92M
- **Yr3 Cash-on-Cash Yield: 15.8%** (if partner retention managed correctly)
- With offshoring (push EBITDA to 38%): $9.4M EBITDA → FCF $8.3M → **16.6% yield**

### Key Risk
- Partner flight → client churn. MUST structure earnouts tied to 2-year client retention
- AI disruption beginning but still 5-8 years before material impact at specialization level
- Use offshoring in Year 2-3 to protect margins from AI before it matters
