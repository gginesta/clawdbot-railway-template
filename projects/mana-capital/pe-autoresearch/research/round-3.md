# Round 3: Stress Test
**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Goal:** Recession, regulatory, operator, and disruption tests; apply penalties/bonuses; cut to top 5

---

## Stress Test Criteria
1. Recession performance (2008-09 and 2020)
2. Regulatory risk in target geographies
3. Customer concentration typical
4. Operator dependency — ease of hiring CEO
5. Technology disruption risk (5-10 year horizon)

---

## Sector-by-Sector Stress Tests

### 1. Pest Control Services

**Recession Test (2008-09):**
- Commercial revenue: only 2.2% decline (Pestmaster Franchise data — confirmed)
- Residential: flat to slightly up (homeowners still needed pest control)
- Overall industry: remained positive even in 2008-09; essential service classification
- 2020 (COVID): Residential demand SURGED (Briostack 2025: "residential demand surged, carrying much of the industry's growth")
- Commercial briefly dipped in March-April 2020 as offices closed, then recovered
- **Verdict: EXCELLENT recession performer. -2.2% worst case commercial; residential buffers fully.**

**Regulatory Risk:**
- Low: Mostly state-level licensing for applicators; no major regulatory risk
- Environmental: Some pressure on specific chemical compounds (organophosphates), but substitutes exist
- LatAm: Brazil has ANVISA pesticide regulations; manageable
- **Verdict: LOW regulatory risk**

**Customer Concentration:**
- Residential: By design very diverse (hundreds to thousands of accounts per operator)
- Commercial: Could be concentrated if 1 large client = 20%+ of revenue — watch for this
- **Verdict: LOW concentration typical**

**Operator Dependency:**
- General managers and route supervisors available; not highly specialized CEO pool
- CEO for a $15M pest control platform: available from within industry or adjacent services
- Salary range: $150-250K/year for a strong GM
- **Verdict: LOW dependency risk; good talent pool**

**Technology Disruption:**
- IoT sensors and AI could reduce inspection labor in 5-10 years
- But: physical treatment (spraying, baiting, trapping) cannot be automated
- AI might improve efficiency but not eliminate the need for service
- Climate change is EXPANDING demand long-term (more pests, longer seasons)
- **Verdict: LOW disruption risk — physical service requirement limits automation**

**Net Adjustment:** +3 points (bonus for recession performance + low disruption)
**Stress-Tested Score: 79.5 + 3 = 82.5**

### 2. Funeral Homes/Death Care

**Recession Test (2008-09):**
- Funeral services are recession-proof at the service level (people die regardless)
- 2008-09: Shift toward more affordable/cremation services reduced average revenue per funeral
- Cremation rate jumped from 36.0% (2008) to 43.2% (2012) — cost-pressure driven
- Net revenue per call can drop during recession; volume holds but per-call pricing compresses
- 2020 (COVID): Major BOOST — excess deaths created elevated demand; COVID deaths significantly increased volume
- SCI stock roughly doubled 2020-2024
- **Verdict: VERY STRONG — volume is resilient, but per-call revenue can decline modestly in recessions**

**Regulatory Risk:**
- US: State funeral board licensing; embalmer, funeral director licenses needed
- Real estate permits for funeral homes (special use zoning)
- LatAm: Different country-by-country regulation; more complex for entry
- APAC: Japan has strict funeral industry regulations; different cultural norms
- **Verdict: MODERATE — mainly licensing. Geographic expansion outside US adds complexity**

**Customer Concentration:**
- Zero — each customer is a one-time transaction; no single customer dependency
- Pre-need contracts create a modest "bank" but no concentration risk
- **Verdict: ZERO concentration risk — best possible**

**Operator Dependency:**
- Licensed funeral directors required by law; state-specific licenses
- Management CEO: Funeral director background helps but not mandatory for CEO
- PE firms (SCI, Carriage) have shown professional management works
- Salary: $200-350K for strong multi-location CEO
- **Verdict: MODERATE dependency — funeral director licensing creates friction, but manageable**

**Technology Disruption:**
- Low: Death care has tried digitization (online memorials, pre-need digital) but the core service is physical
- "Direct cremation" services (Tulip, Neptune) are online-first disruptors targeting low-cost segment
- Risk: If direct cremation continues rising (now 57%+ of deaths), average revenue per call declines
- Mitigation: Position in premium/full-service; older demographics prefer traditional
- **Verdict: MODERATE disruption — cremation trend + online direct cremation. Manageable if positioned correctly.**

**Net Adjustment:** -1 point (moderate licensing complexity + cremation pricing pressure)
**Stress-Tested Score: 76.5 - 1 = 75.5**

### 3. Accounting/CPA Firms

**Recession Test (2008-09):**
- CPA Trendlines 2010: "Recession Flattens Accounting Firms in '09" — headline revenue stayed flat to slightly up
- Tax compliance, audit mandates don't disappear; but discretionary advisory work cut
- Collections became harder — clients slow-paid; AR/WIP worsened
- 2020: Light layoffs; accounting firms fared better than most sectors
- Compliance and tax work continued; M&A activity initially slowed (advisory revenue fell)
- **Verdict: MODERATE — mostly flat, but advisory revenue dips in recessions**

**Regulatory Risk:**
- US: CPA licensing (state-specific); strict practice standards
- UK: ICAEW regulation
- EU: Country-specific but generally stable
- Key trend: Increasingly stringent audit regulations may benefit larger accredited firms
- **Verdict: LOW-MODERATE — regulation favors established players**

**Customer Concentration:**
- Main risk: Mid-sized CPA firms often have 2-3 large clients = 30%+ revenue
- Multi-industry client base mitigates this; check at acquisition
- **Verdict: MODERATE — watch for concentration; buy firms with >20 clients**

**Operator Dependency:**
- This is the KEY risk: Partners often own the client relationships
- When partners leave, clients often follow (40-60% client retention post-departure)
- Mitigation: Earnouts tied to retention; multi-year contracts with clients
- CEO hire: Accounting firm administrators/non-partner CEOs are available but limited
- **Verdict: HIGH dependency risk — primary risk for this sector**

**Technology Disruption:**
- AI is a REAL and PRESENT threat to accounting:
  - QuickBooks AI, Xero, and Sage AI automate bookkeeping
  - AI coding and audit tools emerging from Big 4 labs
  - Tax prep automation (TurboTax AI, H&R Block) threatens mass-market tax compliance
  - But: Complex tax planning, M&A advisory, specialized niches resist automation
- **Verdict: HIGH disruption risk at the commodity end; niche specialization is the defense**

**Net Adjustment:** -5 points (high partner dependency + real AI disruption risk)
**Stress-Tested Score: 72.0 - 5 = 67.0**

### 4. Healthcare RCM

**Recession Test (2008-09):**
- Healthcare spending is relatively recession-resistant
- People still get sick; hospitals don't stop billing
- 2008-09: Some elective procedures deferred, reducing volume; but critical care and billing continued
- 2020: COVID disrupted elective surgery volumes in April-May; bounced back strongly by Q3 2020
- **Verdict: MODERATE-STRONG — brief dips but recovery is fast**

**Regulatory Risk:**
- HIGH: Medicare/Medicaid rules change frequently (CMS regulatory changes)
- ICD coding updates, payer rule changes can disrupt revenue
- HIPAA and data security compliance is complex
- AI use in billing increasingly scrutinized by payers
- **Verdict: MODERATE-HIGH regulatory risk**

**Customer Concentration:**
- Mid-sized RCM firms often have 1-3 hospital systems = 30-60% revenue
- Hospital systems have leverage to negotiate aggressively
- 5-year contracts typical, but renewal uncertainty
- **Verdict: HIGH concentration risk — this is a known weakness**

**Operator Dependency:**
- Needs healthcare billing expertise + coding specialists
- CEO: Healthcare operations background required; pool exists but is not wide
- AI is changing the skill mix — transition risk during integration
- **Verdict: MODERATE-HIGH**

**Technology Disruption:**
- BLACK BOOK SURVEY (April 2025): "Traditional outsourcing firms must undergo radical reinvention or risk obsolescence in an automation-first future"
- AI RCM platforms (Waystar, Experian, Epic AI) are growing fast
- Services-only RCM without tech component faces margin compression
- 5-year window before significant disruption hits lower-tech RCM companies
- **Verdict: HIGH disruption risk — this is the primary investment risk**

**Net Adjustment:** -7 points (AI disruption + concentration + US-only geography)
**Stress-Tested Score: 70.3 - 7 = 63.3**

### 5. Fire Protection/Life Safety

**Recession Test (2008-09):**
- Mandatory inspections continue regardless of economy
- Construction slowdown reduced new installation work; but service/inspection revenue held
- 2020: Essential service designation; buildings still needed inspections
- Insurance requirements enforce compliance regardless of budget pressures
- **Verdict: STRONG — mandatory regulatory requirements shield from recession**

**Regulatory Risk:**
- Regulatory risk works IN FAVOR: Stricter fire codes = more work
- Building code updates create upgrade cycles
- NFPA codes updated regularly → consistent upgrade demand
- **Verdict: POSITIVE regulatory environment**

**Customer Concentration:**
- Commercial customers (office buildings, hospitals, schools, factories)
- Mix of one-time installations and recurring annual inspections
- Single large hospital system or commercial landlord can be 20%+ revenue
- **Verdict: MODERATE — watch at acquisition; diversified customer base is attainable**

**Operator Dependency:**
- Licensed fire protection inspectors and sprinkler fitters
- NICET certifications (National Institute for Certification in Engineering Technologies) required
- CEO from within the industry or related trades (electrical, HVAC)
- Growing competition for tech-enabled talent
- **Verdict: MODERATE — specialty certifications create friction but pool exists**

**Technology Disruption:**
- IoT sensors and AI ENHANCE the business rather than threaten it:
  - Smart fire detection (Breakwater MA article) → generates MORE service work
  - Remote monitoring upgrades create subscription revenue streams
  - AI-powered inspection planning doesn't eliminate the physical inspection requirement
  - Liability requirements mean human-certified inspections still mandated
- **Verdict: LOW-MODERATE — tech enhances rather than disrupts; smart operators can ride the wave**

**Net Adjustment:** +2 points (mandatory compliance shield + tech as tailwind)
**Stress-Tested Score: 68.9 + 2 = 70.9**

### 6. Waste Collection/Environmental

**Recession Test (2008-09):**
- Municipal contracts held (government-backed)
- Commercial waste collection declined slightly with business activity
- 2020: Residential waste up (people at home); commercial briefly down
- Route density improvements possible during consolidation
- **Verdict: STRONG — municipal contracts provide floor; some commercial cyclicality**

**Regulatory Risk:**
- EPA regulations, state environmental compliance
- Strict licensing for transport of hazardous/medical waste
- Climate regulation could mandate new waste management tech
- **Verdict: MODERATE — established rules; new climate mandates possible**

**Customer Concentration:**
- Municipal contracts: Positive (government is lowest default risk); but renegotiations every 3-7 years
- Commercial: More fragmented
- **Verdict: MODERATE — municipal concentration is a feature not a bug**

**Operator Dependency:**
- Fleet management expertise; operational CEO available from logistics/trucking
- Route management software exists (AMCS, Routemaster)
- **Verdict: LOW-MODERATE — operational CEO pool exists in logistics**

**Technology Disruption:**
- Route optimization via AI is underway — REDUCES costs but doesn't threaten the service
- Electric vehicle fleet transition creates capex pressure in 10-year horizon
- Recycling/sustainability tech could redefine service mix but not eliminate it
- **Verdict: LOW disruption risk — physical collection cannot be automated**

**Net Adjustment:** 0 (balanced; no bonus or penalty)
**Stress-Tested Score: 67.7 + 0 = 67.7**
