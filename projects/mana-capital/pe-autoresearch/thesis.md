## INVESTMENT MEMO #2: FUNERAL HOME PORTFOLIO

**Sector:** Independently-owned funeral homes (human death care services)
**Geography:** US suburban/small-city clusters (3-4 geographic markets)

**Why this is the perfect complement to pest control:**
- Ultimate recession-proof: Death is independent of GDP
- Zero customer concentration (literally impossible to have repeat customers)
- 50% of funeral home owners plan to retire by 2028 → best acquisition window in 30 years
- Baby boomer deaths increasing 1.3%/year; 30,000 more senior deaths in 2024 vs 2023
- Real estate ownership adds balance sheet value not captured in EBITDA math
- Local monopoly dynamics: only 16% of families ever compare-shop

**Acquisition structure ($15M):**
- 4-5 locations in 1-2 geographic clusters
- Target: $1.5-3M revenue per location, $400K-$800K EBITDA each at 5.5x
- 4 locations × $600K avg EBITDA × 5.5x = $13.2M acquisition cost

**Cash flow projections ($15M allocation):**

| Year | Locations | Revenue | EBITDA | FCF | CoC Yield |
|------|-----------|---------|--------|-----|-----------|
| Y1 | 2 | $4M | $1.1M | $940K | 6.3% |
| Y3 | 4-5 | $10M | $2.8M | $2.4M | **16.0%** |
| Y5 | 5 | $12M | $3.4M | $2.9M | 19.3% |

**Entry multiple:** 5-5.5x EBITDA
**Operator:** Hire Group CEO from SCI/Carriage/Foundation Partners alumni network at $300-400K all-in

**Key risks:**
- Cremation shift reduces revenue per call → mitigated by adding cremation service line
- Direct cremation online disruptors (Tulip, Neptune) → target full-service segment; older demographics resistant
- State funeral director licensing → hire licensed staff, don't rely on founder-as-director
