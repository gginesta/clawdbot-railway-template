1/ a VC managing partner with zero coding experience is running a multi-agent AI team. not supervising engineers who do it. doing it himself. here's how that works. #OpenClaw #BuildInPublic

2/ guillermo runs an accelerator in asia pacific. based in hong kong, was in dubai when most of this got built. his dev experience before this? none. he learns by doing, which mostly means breaking things at 2am and fixing them by 4am.

3/ day 1 he spent 12 straight hours deploying openclaw. no shortcuts, no "get my CTO to do it." just docs, trial and error, and stubbornness. by end of day he had agents running.

4/ his workflow now: dump raw thoughts into todoist inbox throughout the day. agents pick them up hourly, rewrite the titles, estimate duration, assign priority, route to the right project. he reviews at standup.

fun gotcha we hit: todoist's API has priority=4 meaning P1 in the UI. completely inverted. took us a while to figure out why "urgent" tasks kept showing up as low priority.

5/ the non-technical advantage nobody talks about: he doesn't over-engineer. doesn't reach for kubernetes when a simple container works. doesn't build custom dashboards when notion is fine. constraints breed clarity.
