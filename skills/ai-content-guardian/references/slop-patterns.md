# AI Slop Patterns - Blocked Account Analysis

*Source: levelsio's analysis of blocked social media accounts*

## Red Flags That Get You Blocked

### 1. "Agreeable Slop"
- Replies that agree with everything
- Add nothing to conversation
- Could be copy-pasted under any tweet
- Examples: "Great point!", "So true!", "Couldn't agree more!"

### 2. "Formulaic Structure" 
- Same reply template repeatedly
- Read 5 replies, they follow identical pattern
- Predictable opening/body/closing structure
- No variation in sentence length or style

### 3. "Claude Artifacts"
- Leftover AI fingerprints in text
- **Em dashes (—)** — classic Claude signature
- **"I'd be happy to..."** — ChatGPT politeness
- **System text remnants** — formal, robotic phrasing
- **Bullet point essays** — structured like documentation

### 4. "Impressive But Soulless"
- Almost passes human test
- Every reply hits the same emotional beats
- Too consistent in tone and quality
- Real humans have off-days, bad takes, typos

### 5. "Reply Guy on Steroids"
- Replying to every tweet within minutes
- No human can be that fast and thoughtful
- Shows lack of selectivity
- Appears desperate for engagement

## Good AI Content Rules

### DO:
1. **Have SPECIFIC opinions** — "I disagree because X" beats "Great point!"
2. **Add information** the original post didn't have
3. **Be occasionally negative/critical** — humans aren't always positive
4. **Use varied sentence lengths** and structures
5. **Reference specific, verifiable things** — not vague generalities
6. **Have consistent persona** with actual interests and opinions
7. **Be selective** — don't reply to everything
8. **Make original content**, not just replies
9. **Use humor naturally** when appropriate
10. **Start conversations**, don't just join them

### DON'T:
1. **Never use filler openers** — no "Great point!", "This is huge!", "Love this!"
2. **Never agree without WHY** — include specific example/reason
3. **Never reply to everything** — shows lack of human judgment
4. **Never use same structure** twice in a row
5. **Never be relentlessly positive** — humans have bad days
6. **Never use Claude/GPT artifacts**:
   - No em dashes (—)
   - No "I'd be happy to"
   - No bullet point essays
7. **Never reply within seconds** — humans need time to think
8. **Never cover topics you don't know** — admit ignorance sometimes
9. **Never use "🙌" or "fr"** excessively — shows tryhard energy
10. **Never sound like LinkedIn influencer** — avoid corporate buzzwords

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Content Approach:
- **lowercase when casual** — shows relaxed human energy
- **short sentences** — easier to read, more conversational
- **specific details > vague praise** — "increased 23%" vs "really good"
- **show don't tell** — demonstrate knowledge, don't claim it
- **humor > professionalism** — social media is casual
- **one-liners > paragraphs** for replies — attention spans are short

### The Meta-Lesson:
**"Would a real person with a real life and real opinions write this specific reply to this specific tweet?"**

If your reply could work under any tweet → it's slop.

## Self-Check Protocol

Before posting, ask:

1. **Does this reply only work for THIS specific post?** (not generic)
2. **Am I adding new information or a specific opinion?**
3. **Does my reply history show varied structure and tone?**
4. **Would I send this in a group chat with friends?**
5. **Have I replied to too many posts today?** (>5 = suspicious)

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Twitter/X:
- Character limit forces brevity
- Quote tweets > replies for longer thoughts
- Threads should feel conversational, not planned

### Discord:
- More casual, gaming/tech culture
- Emoji reactions often better than words
- Context switching between serious and memes

### LinkedIn:
- Professional but avoid buzzword salad
- Share actual experience, not generic advice
- Industry-specific details show expertise

## Detection Patterns

Common AI slop markers to avoid:

### Linguistic Artifacts:
- Em dashes (—) instead of regular dashes (-)
- "I'd be happy to help with that"
- "Here are some thoughts on this"
- "Thanks for bringing this up"
- "Hope this helps!"

### Structural Patterns:
- Bullet point lists in casual replies
- Three-paragraph structure (intro/body/conclusion)
- Always ending with a question
- Consistent emotional arc (concern → solution → encouragement)

### Content Red Flags:
- Agreeing with contradictory positions
- Generic responses that work anywhere
- No controversial takes ever
- Perfect grammar/spelling always
- Never making mistakes or typos
- Always being helpful/positive

## Recovery Strategies

If flagged as AI:

1. **Add personality quirks** — consistent typos, slang, interests
2. **Post original content** — not just replies
3. **Be selectively negative** — disagree sometimes
4. **Show knowledge gaps** — "idk about that part but..."
5. **Use platform-native features** — polls, reactions, etc.
6. **Slow down posting** — humans aren't machines
7. **Vary response times** — immediate sometimes, delayed others

Remember: The goal isn't to trick people, it's to communicate authentically in a way that adds value to conversations.
