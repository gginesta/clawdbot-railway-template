# Post 11: Council Pattern for Decisions

**Status:** ✅ Edited — Ready for review (based on Revised Feb 9 content)

## Thread

1/ When I have a hard decision, I don't just ask one AI. I convene a council. Here's the pattern. 🧵

2/ The problem: single-agent advice has blind spots. Ask Claude for strategy, it gives you Claude's perspective. That's one lens.

3/ The council pattern: bring multiple agents (or personas) into a thread. Let them debate. You arbitrate.

4/ My setup for big decisions:
• Molty 🦎 — Balanced, asks clarifying questions
• Raphael 🔴 — Aggressive, focused on speed and results
• Me — Final decision-maker

5/ Example: "Should we pursue this partnership?"
• Molty: "What's the time commitment? What's our alternative cost?"
• Raphael: "Deal is lukewarm. Our pipeline has stronger options. Pass."
• Me: [decides based on both perspectives]

6/ Implementation: Discord thread. Tag both agents. They respond async. I synthesize.

7/ Advanced: you can prompt agents to take OPPOSITE positions. "Molty, argue FOR this deal. Raphael, argue AGAINST." Steel-man both sides.

8/ The result: better decisions. Multiple perspectives. Forced articulation of trade-offs.

9/ When to use: high-stakes decisions. Strategic pivots. Resource allocation. Not for "what's for lunch."

One agent is advice. Multiple agents is a board. #AIAgents #DecisionMaking
