## Thread

1/ I'm a non-technical managing partner. Last week I ran 2 AI agents 24/7 for my actual job. Here's what I learned about cost, reliability, and sanity. 🧵

2/ The setup: Molty 🦎 (coordinator agent) handles my inbox, calendar, Discord. Raphael 🔴 (corporate agent) drafts proposals, monitors deal flow. Both run on Railway.

3/ Cost breakdown after 7 days:
• Railway hosting: $12
• Claude API (Sonnet): $47
• Total: ~$59/week ($8.40/day)

That's cheaper than one hour of a junior employee.

4/ What surprised me: the agents work best when they DON'T try to be smart. Simple rules, clear handoffs, explicit memory files. No magic.

5/ Biggest fail: I gave Molty a vague instruction ("help me with emails"). It hallucinated context and sent a weird reply. Lesson: specificity > speed.

6/ What works: daily standups. Every day at 5PM, Molty summarizes what happened, what's pending, asks for decisions. Forces me to stay in the loop.

7/ The real unlock isn't automation—it's leverage. I'm doing the same work but with 2 extra brains that don't forget, don't get tired, and cost $8/day.

8/ Will I keep going? Yes. The ROI is obvious. Next: adding Leonardo (research agent) and Donatello (dev agent).
