## Thread

1/ Before building AI agents, I spent 2 weeks just auditing my own workflows. Best decision I made. Here's why. 🧵

2/ The temptation: jump straight into building. "I'll automate email!" But automate WHAT exactly? Most people can't articulate their actual workflows.

3/ The audit process: for 2 weeks, I logged every task I did. Time, tools used, inputs, outputs. No automation—just observation.

4/ What I found:
• 40% of my time: reviewing documents and writing feedback
• 25%: scheduling and coordination
• 20%: research and due diligence
• 15%: random admin

5/ The insight: I didn't need a "general assistant" agent. I needed specialists for each bucket.

6/ Audit → Agent mapping:
• Document review → Raphael (structured feedback)
• Coordination → Molty (calendar, discord, reminders)
• Research → Leonardo (coming soon)

7/ What NOT to automate (yet): high-stakes decisions, relationship-building, creative strategy. Some things need the human.

8/ The framework: if a task is repetitive AND low-stakes AND clearly defined → automate it. Otherwise, wait.

Start with audit, not ambition. You'll build better systems. #AIAgents #BuildInPublic
