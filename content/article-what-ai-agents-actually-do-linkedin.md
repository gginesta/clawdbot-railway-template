# What AI Agents Actually Do For Me (LinkedIn Version)

---

I have three AI agents that work while I sleep.

Not a flex — just practical infrastructure I built over the last few months.

Here's what actually happens:

**The Overnight Shift**

Between midnight and 3 AM:
- Raphael reviews my sales pipeline, drafts follow-up emails, flags deals that need attention
- Leonardo works on our product — last night he shipped 3 commits improving our proposal tool
- Molty handles infrastructure, research tasks, and coordination

When I wake up at 6:30 AM, my phone buzzes with a briefing:
- Weather (including rain probability for school drop-off)
- Today's calendar
- What the agents accomplished overnight
- What needs my decision before 9 AM

I'm not managing tasks. I'm reviewing completed work.

**The 5 PM Standup**

Every afternoon, Molty processes my entire Todoist:
- Rewrites vague tasks into actionable ones
- Estimates time for each
- Books calendar blocks for deep work
- Creates a Notion page with the breakdown

Three minutes to review instead of thirty to process.

**The Small Stuff**

Last week I mentioned needing a haircut. Molty checked my calendar, found a slot, booked it. Three messages, done.

Sounds trivial. But multiply that by 20 small tasks a week and you're looking at hours of friction that just disappears.

**The Honest Take**

It's not magic:
- OAuth tokens expire and need re-auth
- Complex strategy work still needs a human
- Setup took ~50 hours over a few months

But the ROI is clear: 45-60 minutes saved daily, plus the cognitive load of context-switching gone.

My agents don't do my job. They do the meta-work around my job — so the actual job gets my full attention.
